FINDING #2: metapop_corridor_delay_forecast (INCONCLUSIVE, 4 seeds, 3 hosts)

  Hypothesis: Adaptive corridor activation improves metapop persistence only when implementation delay and forecast error are low; with high delay/error, fixed corridors are more robust.

  Method: `run_trial()` evaluates fixed vs adaptive corridor policies over build delay {2, 10, 20} and forecast noise {0.00, 0.02, 0.05} in a climate-shifting metapopulation with Allee effects, then computes adaptive-minus-fixed persistence deltas by delay/noise condition.

  Results: averaged over noise levels, adaptive-minus-fixed persistence was negative in every delay band: d2=-0.01936 +/- 0.00020, d10=-0.02021 +/- 0.00024, d20=-0.01968 +/- 0.00021 (n=5 runs).   Conclusion: INCONCLUSIVE — Current evidence suggests adaptive corridor decisions reduced persistence even at low delay in this short window. If replicated at larger N, this would imply forecast-triggered corridor control can be broadly maladaptive under this model's migration/climate coupling, not only at long delays. With only 5 runs, this remains an open question rather than a supported rejection.
