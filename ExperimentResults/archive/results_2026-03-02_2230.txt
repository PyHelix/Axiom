NEW EXPERIMENT: combined_compositionality.py
=============================================
RATIONALE: Findings #36 (ortho helps modestly) and #37 (bottleneck helps strongly)
establish that both interventions independently improve compositional generalization.
The natural next question is: do they combine synergistically?

Design:
  - Architecture: [input(20), W, B, W, output(2)] — same as bottleneck experiment
  - 4 intervention conditions:
    1. None (baseline: B=W, ortho_lambda=0)
    2. Bottleneck only (B<W, ortho_lambda=0)
    3. Ortho only (B=W, ortho_lambda=0.1)
    4. Both combined (B<W, ortho_lambda=0.1)
  - 3 widths: [64, 128, 256]
  - 3 bottleneck settings: [16, 32, "none"]
  - 2 ortho settings: [0.0, 0.1]
  - Total: 18 configurations per seed
  - Same compositional task (20 features, 4 groups, holdout OOD)

Key questions this experiment answers:
  Q1: Is the combined effect additive or superadditive (synergy)?
  Q2: Does combined intervention outperform either intervention alone?
  Q3: What is the hierarchy: combined >> bottleneck > ortho > none?

If synergy is detected, this would strengthen the case that:
  - Bottleneck constrains dimensionality (hard architectural constraint)
  - Ortho prevents eigenvalue collapse within each layer (soft regularization)
  - Together they address different aspects of dimensional collapse
  This would constitute a publishable finding on intervention complementarity.
