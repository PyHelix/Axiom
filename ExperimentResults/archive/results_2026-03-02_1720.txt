   Rank/width ratios by network width (mean +/- std):
     Width 32:  0.672 +/- 0.027

     Width 256: 0.394 +/- 0.014

   ALL 14 seeds show strict monotonic decrease (W32 > W64 > W128 > W256).
   Very low variance across seeds — highly reproducible.
   This finding explains the Width-Generalization Paradox: wider networks learn
   lower-rank representations (relative to capacity), which concentrates information
   into fewer dimensions, explaining both high CKA similarity (#28) and poor
   compositional generalization (#31).
