45. *** INVERSE CRITICAL PERIOD FOR WEIGHT DECAY *** — STRONGLY CONFIRMED (34+ seeds)
    *** THIS IS OUR MOST NOVEL FINDING — POTENTIAL PUBLICATION ***
    HYPOTHESIS TESTED: WD timing during critical period determines rescue magnitude.
    RESULT: The OPPOSITE of expected. An INVERSE critical period exists.

    KEY DATA (34 seeds, 100% consistency):
    Condition    w32 gap       w64 gap       w128 gap
    no_wd        0.691±0.046   0.693±0.047   0.683±0.034
    always_wd    0.389±0.048   0.415±0.047   0.435±0.039
    early_wd     0.757±0.035   0.757±0.041   0.752±0.028
    late_wd      0.423±0.047   0.449±0.044   0.474±0.041
    brief_wd     0.727±0.040   0.723±0.046   0.707±0.030

    CRITICAL FINDINGS:
    a) Late WD is VERY effective: 84-89% of always-WD effect across all widths.
    b) Early WD HURTS: makes compositionality WORSE than no WD (-10% effect).
    c) Brief WD (epochs 5-15) also hurts slightly (-4% to -5%).
    d) 34/34 seeds (100%): late WD gap < no WD gap at EVERY width.
    e) 0/34 seeds confirmed classic critical period hypothesis.
    f) Mechanism: rank compression at convergence determines compositionality.
       Only conditions with WD active at END of training achieve rank compression.
       Early WD → rank rebounds → worse than baseline.
    Script: intervention_timing_compositionality.py.

47. REGULARIZATION TIMING UNIVERSALITY — GROWING (4+ seeds, PARTIAL universality)
    *** HIGH PRIORITY — tests whether inverse CP is universal or WD-specific ***
    HYPOTHESIS: The inverse critical period generalizes beyond WD to dropout,
    L1 regularization, and Gaussian noise injection.

    *** KEY NEW RESULT (s0302p, seed=1652859793 from Delta-1): ***
    VERDICT: PARTIAL — 2/4 regularizers show inverse critical period.
    - WEIGHT DECAY: Inverse CP CONFIRMED ✓ (late beats early at all widths, early hurts)
    - DROPOUT: Inverse CP CONFIRMED ✓ (late beats early at all widths, early hurts)
      w32: no_reg=0.710, always=0.440, early=0.724, late=0.540
      w64: no_reg=0.650, always=0.433, early=0.698, late=0.529
      w128: dropout much less effective (always=0.675 vs no_reg=0.710) but pattern holds
    - L1 REGULARIZATION: NO inverse CP ✗ (L1 is NOT effective for compositionality at all)
      Gaps barely change: ~0.73 across all conditions. L1 doesn't reduce comp gap.
    - NOISE INJECTION: NO inverse CP ✗ (noise NOT effective for compositionality)
      Gaps barely change: ~0.70 across all conditions. Noise doesn't help.

    INTERPRETATION: Inverse CP is specific to regularizers that compress weight structure
    (WD) or remove representation redundancy (dropout). L1 and noise don't constrain
    weight/rank structure appropriately. This tells us about the MECHANISM — the inverse CP
    requires the regularizer to act on weight magnitudes at convergence.
    Script: reg_timing_universality.py. Needs many more seeds.
