33. NEURON SPECIALIZATION — CONFIRMED, HYPOTHESIS REFUTED (275 seeds)
    FINDING: Wider nets produce MORE selective neurons (selectivity increases

    Effective dim ratio PLUMMETS: 0.281→0.071 from W32→W256.
    Wide nets use only 7% of dimensional capacity. Problem is not redundancy.

34. PRUNING COMPOSITIONALITY — DEFINITIVELY CONFIRMED NEGATIVE & RETIRED (48 seeds)
    FINDING: Magnitude pruning does NOT recover compositional generalization.
    OOD accuracy ~0% across all prune rates and widths. Training dynamics issue.

35. REGULARIZED COMPOSITIONALITY — STRONGLY CONFIRMED (538 seeds, 9+ hosts)
    FINDING: Weight decay (0.05) reduces compositional gap at ALL widths.
    Dropout shows asymmetric width interaction: helps narrow not wide.
    W32: gap 0.693→0.321, W64: 0.685→0.390, W128: 0.678→0.404, W256: 0.676→0.414.
    Best reg: dropout_0.4 for W32, wd_0.05 for W64-W256 (100% dominant).

36. ORTHOGONALITY COMPOSITIONALITY — STRONGLY CONFIRMED (255 seeds)
    FINDING: Soft orthogonality penalty rescues compositionality (100% True).
    ortho_helps_wide_networks: 71.4% True (182/255) — UP from 60% with more data.
    ortho_increases_effective_rank: 99.2% True (253/255). Effect modest vs bottleneck.

37. BOTTLENECK COMPOSITIONALITY — DEFINITIVELY CONFIRMED (~280+ seeds, 98%)
    FINDING: Bottleneck layer [W->B->W] STRONGLY rescues compositionality.
    Gap reduction: 4-8% absolute. Best: w128_b16, w256_b32.
    FIRST INTERVENTION THAT REVERSES WIDTH-COMPOSITIONALITY TRADEOFF.
    Hierarchy: bottleneck >> ortho >> dropout >> pruning(none).

38. COMBINED COMPOSITIONALITY — GROWING (124 seeds)
    FINDING: Combining bottleneck+ortho is usually beneficial (75.8%).
    Synergy detected 82.3% (102/124). Previous 90% was a batch artifact.
    Effect types: superadditive=41.1%, subadditive=34.7%, approx_additive=24.2%.
    Interaction genuinely variable across seeds — not consistently one type.
    Bottleneck remains the dominant component.
