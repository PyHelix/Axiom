45. *** INVERSE CRITICAL PERIOD FOR WEIGHT DECAY *** — STRONGLY CONFIRMED (34+ seeds)
    *** THIS IS OUR MOST NOVEL FINDING — POTENTIAL PUBLICATION ***
    HYPOTHESIS TESTED: WD timing during critical period determines rescue magnitude.
    RESULT: The OPPOSITE of expected. An INVERSE critical period exists.

    KEY DATA (34 seeds, 100% consistency):
    Condition    w32 gap       w64 gap       w128 gap
    no_wd        0.691±0.046   0.693±0.047   0.683±0.034
    always_wd    0.389±0.048   0.415±0.047   0.435±0.039
    early_wd     0.757±0.035   0.757±0.041   0.752±0.028
    late_wd      0.423±0.047   0.449±0.044   0.474±0.041
    brief_wd     0.727±0.040   0.723±0.046   0.707±0.030

    CRITICAL FINDINGS:
    a) Late WD is VERY effective: 84-89% of always-WD effect across all widths.
    b) Early WD HURTS: makes compositionality WORSE than no WD (-10% effect).
    c) Brief WD (epochs 5-15) also hurts slightly (-4% to -5%).
    d) 34/34 seeds (100%): late WD gap < no WD gap at EVERY width.
    e) 0/34 seeds confirmed classic critical period hypothesis.
    f) Mechanism: rank compression at convergence determines compositionality.
       Only conditions with WD active at END of training achieve rank compression.
       Early WD → rank rebounds → worse than baseline.
    Script: intervention_timing_compositionality.py.
