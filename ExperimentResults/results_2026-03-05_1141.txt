FINDING #1: collatz_evt_tails (CONFIRMED — Weibull not Gumbel, 190 results, 18 hosts)

  Hypothesis: Block-maxima of Collatz stopping times follow a Gumbel extreme value
  distribution, with location scaling as c1*log(B) + c2*log(start).

  Method: Computes block-maxima of Collatz stopping times across 200 blocks of varying
  size (100-10000), fits L-moment ratios (tau3 L-skewness) to classify EVT domain, and
  regresses location parameter against log(block_size).

  Results:
  - Mean tau3 (L-skewness) = 0.1117, Gumbel theoretical = 0.1699
  - Deviation from Gumbel: Cohen's d = 10.67, t = -147.1
  - EVT domain: 190/190 classified as Weibull (100%)
  - Location ~ log(block_size) R^2 = 0.9962 (confirmed 190/190)
  - Mean log slope = 42.55 +/- 0.43
  - Cross-host ICC(tau3) = 0.18

  Conclusion: REJECTED (Gumbel) / CONFIRMED (Weibull + log scaling) — Collatz stopping
  time block-maxima fall in the Weibull (bounded/light-tailed) extreme value domain, not
  Gumbel as hypothesized. The L-skewness is massively below the Gumbel value with d=10.67
  across 18 independent hosts. This indicates the tails of stopping time distributions are
  lighter than exponential — there is an effective upper bound on how fast block-maxima grow.
  Meanwhile, the location parameter scales precisely as log(block_size) (R^2 > 0.996),
  confirming logarithmic growth of extremes. This constrains the tail behavior of Collatz
  sequences: despite unbounded stopping times, their extremes grow slowly and are more
  bounded than classical EVT would predict.

FINDING #2: minority_game_memory_heterogeneity (CONFIRMED, 127 results, 11 hosts)

  Hypothesis: Heterogeneous agent memory distributions (bimodal, power-law, uniform-random)
  alter the phase transition in the minority game compared to uniform memory.

  Method: N=201 agents, 2 strategies each, 500 rounds. Four memory distributions (uniform,
  bimodal, power-law, uniform-random) crossed with 5 dominant memory values (M=2-6).
  10,517 total trials.

  Results:
  - Uniform: sharp phase transition (13.5x volatility ratio), min volatility = 0.78
  - Bimodal: compressed transition (2.5x ratio), min volatility = 1.23
  - Power-law: transition destroyed (1.01x ratio), volatility ~ 4.5 constant
  - Uniform-random: transition destroyed (1.00x ratio), volatility ~ 1.22 constant
  - Effect sizes d = 10-35 between distributions at matched M
  - Cross-host CV = 1.1-1.7% across 11 hosts

  Conclusion: CONFIRMED — Memory heterogeneity fundamentally reshapes the minority game
  phase diagram. Power-law (heavy-tailed) heterogeneity completely destroys the phase
  transition by pinning agents at short effective memory (~1.8), locking the system in the
  inefficient crowded regime regardless of nominal memory. Uniform-random heterogeneity
  also eliminates the transition but converges to moderate volatility (1.22), acting as a
  natural regularizer. This reveals that the classic minority game phase transition is
  fragile — it depends critically on agents having identical information capacity. Real
  markets with heterogeneous trader sophistication should not exhibit the sharp
  efficient-to-crowded transition predicted by the uniform-memory model.

FINDING #3: spatial_pgg_punishment_criticality (CONFIRMED first-order, 153 results, 16 hosts)

  Hypothesis: The cooperation-to-defection transition in a spatial public goods game with
  pool punishment is continuous and in the directed percolation (DP) universality class
  (gamma/nu ~ 1.595 in 2D).

  Method: Spatial PGG on 2D grids (L=20,30,50) with defector/cooperator/punisher strategies.
  Fermi imitation dynamics. Sweeps 3 punishment costs x 15 fine ratios. Measures
  susceptibility, Binder cumulants, finite-size scaling exponent gamma/nu.

  Results:
  - gamma/nu = 1.827 +/- 0.204 (n=153)
  - vs DP prediction (1.595): d = 1.14, strongly rejected
  - vs Ising prediction (1.75): d = 0.38, closer but still significantly different
  - First-order signal: 100% of workunits (cooperation jump ~ 0.988)
  - Binder crossing: 100% of workunits
  - Cross-host SD = 0.131 (reproducible)

  Conclusion: REJECTED (DP universality) / CONFIRMED (first-order transition) — The
  cooperation phase transition under pool punishment is discontinuous, not continuous as
  hypothesized. The cooperation fraction jumps by ~0.99 at the critical fine ratio,
  characteristic of a first-order transition. This supports the theoretical prediction that
  punishment creates a coordination game with multiple stable equilibria, producing
  bistability and hysteresis rather than a smooth critical point.

FINDING #4: levy_foraging_renewal_phase (REJECTED, 168 results, 17 hosts)

  Hypothesis: The optimal Levy flight exponent increases toward Brownian (mu > 2) as
  resource renewal rate increases, with a sharp phase transition.

  Method: Single forager performing Levy flights on 200x200 2D torus with 30 resource
  patches (radius 5). Nine Levy exponents (mu=1.1-3.0) crossed with 9 renewal rates
  (0.0-0.5). 7,024 total trials.

  Results:
  - Optimal mu = ~1.14 at ALL renewal rates (most superdiffusive tested)
  - frac_levy_optimal = 1.0, frac_brownian_optimal = 0.0 at every condition
  - Predicted upward shift; observed slight downward (d = -0.44, magnitude -0.010)
  - Cross-host SD = 0.007-0.013 across 17 hosts

  Conclusion: REJECTED — No phase transition from Levy-optimal to Brownian-optimal foraging
  exists at these parameters. With 30 patches on a 200x200 grid (~0.06% coverage), patches
  are sparse enough that long-range ballistic flights always outperform local diffusion.
  The Levy foraging hypothesis predicts a transition only when resources are dense enough
  that exploitation outweighs search — this regime is not reached here.

FINDING #5: oscillatory_roughchannel_lbm_resonance (REJECTED, 120 results, 20 hosts)

  Hypothesis: Intermediate oscillatory forcing period creates a transport-efficiency
  resonance stronger in long-correlation rough channels.

  Method: 2D lattice Boltzmann simulation of flow through rough channels. Crosses 2
  roughness correlations (3, 11) x 3 forcing periods (20, 50, 120) x 2 amplitudes.

  Results:
  - Resonance excess: -62.9 (corr11), -68.6 (corr3) — both strongly negative
  - No resonance peak at intermediate period
  - Forcing always reduces efficiency vs unforced (d = 7-40)
  - Correlation length difference: d = 0.92 (long-corr slightly less damaged)
  - Consistent across 20 hosts

  Conclusion: REJECTED — No transport resonance at intermediate forcing periods. Oscillatory
  forcing monotonically degrades transport efficiency. Rough channel walls dissipate
  oscillatory momentum through boundary layer effects regardless of period. The hypothesized
  resonance mechanism — matching forcing period to channel transit time — does not overcome
  viscous losses.

FINDING #6: metapop_allee_corridor_hysteresis (REJECTED, 288 results, 9 hosts)

  Hypothesis: Control fatigue creates a re-entrant optimum in corridor-trigger threshold
  under Allee risk.

  Method: 42-patch metapopulation with Allee dynamics, climate stress, corridor dispersal.
  Sweeps 5 theta values x 3 fatigue levels. ~3.2M total trials.

  Results:
  - Reentrant support rate: 5.6%
  - Theta shift with fatigue: -0.0157 (opposite predicted direction)
  - Best theta: 0.69 (no fatigue) vs 0.67 (high fatigue) — minimal
  - Cross-host CV = 1.05%

  Conclusion: REJECTED — Control fatigue does not create a re-entrant optimum. The optimal
  corridor threshold is ~0.67-0.69 regardless of fatigue because corridor usage is already
  intermittent enough that fatigue rarely accumulates to consequential levels. The mid-range
  optimum is an intrinsic Allee-corridor property, not fatigue-dependent.

FINDING #7: contact_process_temporal_network (PARTIAL, 120 results, 10 hosts)

  Hypothesis: The quenched-to-annealed crossover in epidemic dynamics depends on network
  topology, with BA networks showing a sharper crossover.

  Method: SIS contact process on temporal networks (N=200, mean degree 6). Three topologies
  (ER, BA, WS) x 7 switching rates x 8 infection rates.

  Results:
  - Topology dominates: BA prevalence ~0.24, ER ~0.10, WS ~0.005 at beta=0.02
  - Degree variance: BA=30.0, ER=4.9, WS=0.6
  - Switching rate effect: small, monotonic (ER d=5.9 max, no sharp crossover)
  - Cross-host max difference = 0.0077

  Conclusion: PARTIAL — Topology strongly determines epidemic thresholds (BA < ER < WS),
  confirming heterogeneous mean-field predictions. The predicted sharp quenched-to-annealed
  crossover is absent at N=200 — switching rate produces only small monotonic prevalence
  increases. The crossover is likely asymptotic, requiring larger networks.
