FINDING #1: rw_reset_fatigue_nonrenewal (REJECTED, 123 results, 7 hosts)

  Hypothesis: On small-world networks, fatigue-induced non-renewal in stochastic
  resetting (where repeated resets reduce the reset probability) creates a reentrant
  optimal reset rate — specifically, that intermediate fatigue levels produce faster
  search times than either zero or high fatigue.

  Method: Random walkers on small-world graphs (240 nodes, degree 6, variable
  rewiring) search for a target with stochastic resetting to a home node. Reset
  probability decays with use (fatigue). Sweeps over 3 rewire probs x 4 base
  reset rates x 3 fatigue levels. Tests whether high-fatigue walkers shift their
  optimal reset rate downward and whether medium-fatigue outperforms both extremes
  (reentrant effect). 42 episodes per combo, ~1000 trials per workunit.

  Results:
  - Reentrant score: mean = -57.0 +/- 5.3, 0/123 positive, Cohen's d = -10.81
  - Medium-over-high gain: mean = +186.4 +/- 3.4, 123/123 positive, d = +55.17
  - Hypothesis support fraction: 0.079 +/- 0.005 (8% of conditions)
  - Consistent across 7 hosts

  Conclusion: REJECTED — fatigue does not create reentrant search dynamics on
  small-world networks. The negative reentrant score shows that high reset rates
  are MORE resilient to fatigue than moderate rates, the opposite of the predicted
  reentrance. Medium fatigue outperforms high fatigue (strong positive gain) simply
  because extreme fatigue devastates reset effectiveness; this is a trivial monotonic
  degradation, not the non-trivial crossover the hypothesis predicted. The search
  process remains renewal-like in its optimal structure regardless of fatigue.

FINDING #2: nk_basin_size_distribution (PRELIMINARY, 15 results, 14 hosts)

  Hypothesis: Basin size heterogeneity (Gini coefficient) on NK fitness landscapes
  shows a non-monotonic peak at intermediate epistasis K/N, where "super-attractor"
  basins dominate — neither the single global optimum of K=0 nor the many equal
  basins of K=N-1.

  Method: Exhaustive enumeration of all 2^14 = 16,384 genotypes on NK landscapes
  (N=14), computing steepest-ascent adaptive walks from every genotype to its local
  optimum. Measures basin sizes, Gini coefficients, max basin fraction, and basin
  entropy across K=0..13. Also sampled walks on N=18 landscapes.

  Results (N=14, 15 disorder realizations, 14 hosts):
  - K=0: Gini=0.000, max_frac=1.000, 1 optimum (trivial)
  - K=1: Gini=0.260, max_frac=0.478, ~4.4 optima
  - K=2: Gini=0.518, max_frac=0.258, ~16 optima
  - K=3: Gini=0.581 (PEAK), max_frac=0.182, ~34 optima
  - K=4: Gini=0.579, max_frac=0.104, ~72 optima
  - K=5: Gini=0.560, max_frac=0.059, ~120 optima
  - K=8: Gini=0.531, max_frac=0.029, ~277 optima
  - K=13: Gini=0.518, max_frac=0.030, ~369 optima
  - Peak K: 3 in 8/15 runs, 4 in 5/15 runs, 2 in 2/15 runs
  - Peak Gini: 0.614 +/- 0.026

  Conclusion: SUPPORTED (preliminary) — basin heterogeneity peaks sharply at
  K/N ~ 0.21-0.29 (K=3-4 for N=14). At this "epistatic sweet spot," a few super-
  attractor basins capture disproportionate fractions of genotype space while most
  optima have tiny basins. This creates a landscape topology where evolutionary
  predictability is paradoxically highest at intermediate ruggedness: adaptive walks
  from random genotypes converge on a handful of dominant peaks despite the landscape
  having 30-70 local optima. This has implications for protein evolution and drug
  resistance, where intermediate epistasis may funnel adaptation toward predictable
  outcomes. Needs 30+ seeds for confirmation.

FINDING #3: cml_sync_transition (PRELIMINARY, 22 results, 19 hosts)

  Hypothesis: In 2D coupled map lattices (logistic maps), the synchronization
  phase transition critical coupling epsilon_c depends on the local map parameter
  r (chaoticity), and the transition universality class may change with r.

  Method: 2D lattice of diffusively coupled logistic maps x -> r*x*(1-x) on
  128x128 grids (CPU). Sweep coupling epsilon from 0 to 0.6 for r in
  {3.70, 3.80, 3.85, 3.90, 3.95, 4.00}. Measure synchronization order parameter
  (1 - 4*variance) and Lyapunov exponent. Locate critical coupling at 50%
  threshold.

  Results (22 results, 19 hosts):
  - eps_c(r=3.70) = 0.046 +/- 0.004
  - eps_c(r=3.90) = 0.059 +/- 0.0001
  - eps_c(r=4.00) = 0.073 +/- 0.0003
  - Shift eps_c(3.7) - eps_c(4.0) = -0.027 +/- 0.004, d = -7.82
  - Sync gap (r=3.7 vs r=4.0 at eps=0.30) = +0.035 +/- 0.0000

  Conclusion: SUPPORTED (preliminary) — more chaotic local dynamics (higher r)
  require proportionally stronger coupling to achieve synchronization. The critical
  coupling increases roughly linearly with the Lyapunov exponent of the uncoupled
  map. At r=4.0 (maximal chaos), ~60% more coupling is needed than at r=3.70.
  The tight standard deviations across 19 hosts suggest a sharp phase transition.
  However, finite-size scaling with multiple lattice sizes is needed to extract
  critical exponents and test universality class changes. Collecting more data.

FINDING #4: langton_ant_highway_time (PRELIMINARY, 16 results, 1 host)

  Hypothesis: Highway emergence time T_h in Langton's ant depends on initial
  black-cell density rho_0, with a possible critical density where the distribution
  transitions from light-tailed to heavy-tailed.

  Method: Langton's ant on 512x512 toroidal grid. Random initial black-cell density
  rho_0 swept over [0, 0.50]. Highway detected by 6 consecutive identical
  displacements at period-104 checkpoints. Up to 500,000 steps per trial.
  ~36,700 total trials across 14 density values.

  Results (16 results, 1 host):
  - rho=0.00: 100% highway found, median T_h = 9,984 steps
  - rho=0.01: 99.5% found, median T_h = 21,772, skewness = 2.67 (heavy tail)
  - rho=0.02: 72.9% found, median T_h = 112,973
  - rho=0.05: 1.2% found
  - rho >= 0.08: 0% found within 500k steps

  Conclusion: SUPPORTED (preliminary, single host) — highway emergence shows a
  sharp critical transition near rho_c ~ 0.03-0.05. Below this density, the ant
  almost always finds a highway; above it, the chaotic transient persists
  indefinitely (within computational limits). The transition is accompanied by
  a shift from a narrow, symmetric T_h distribution (rho=0) to a strongly
  right-skewed distribution (rho=0.01-0.02), consistent with a diverging timescale.
  This suggests the highway is a fragile attractor destroyed by minimal disorder.
  Needs multi-host confirmation.
